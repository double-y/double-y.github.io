What you can learn from project learning

January 17, 2016

One day, I went to an event. It was held by Mission Bit, NPO which teaches programming in a public school. 6 or 7 teams made presentations about what they had made. Most of them had 3 or 4 members but there were a few people who made a service by themselves. The number of people who came to this event was about 60. It means that many people are interested in programming education.

First, they made their own booths and people visited them. They showed their products and talked about how they made it.

After that, each team made a presentation about the products. Most of them were surprisingly good. They were confident and very good at drawing audiences’ attention. When I was in junior high, I couldn’t do such presentations.

This kind of opportunities must be very good for children. It’s not only they can learn programming. Doing projects which you make something in a group is an essential experience. What you can do by yourself is really limited. To archive something valuable, you have work as a team. To do so, you have to break down what to do and assign your teammates what they are good at. Therefore, ability to separate problems well is really important. It also enable them to think clearly, simply and effectively because it’s the process of making things easy to understand.

As a conclusion, programming learning as a project is one of very good method to make children smart. I also learned how to organize my thought through such experience and I think it’s the most effective way. I hope more children can have such opportunities.
